## Research Projects

##### Sample Depth Measurement for Object Data: Graphs, Images, and Functions
- *Supervisor: Dr Anna Calissano (Univeristy College London)*
- Focus on exploring and applying statistical depth measures, such as Tukey’s Depth and Metric Halfspace Depth, to complex object data types, including graphs, images, and functional data.
- Aim to understand the extension of depth concepts to non-Euclidean settings, evaluate their performance on simulated and real data, and build a visualization tool for the depths of object data.

##### Differences between the Markowitz Model and the Index Model under Different Constraints
- *Supervisor: Prof Alexei Chekhlov (Columbia University)* 
> [The Research on the Differences between the Markowitz Model and the Index Model under Different Constraints](https://doi.org/10.54254/2754-1169/2024.19237) \
> *Advances in Economics, Management, and Political Sciences, 139(1): 98-110.* 
- Studied the Modern Portfolio Theory, which includes the Markowitz model, and the Single Index Model.
- Utilized Python to compute and compare the risks and returns of a portfolio consisting of 10 self-selected U.S. stocks predicted by the Markowitz model and the Index Model.

##### Predicting the Stock Opening Price of Apple Company

##### Computing for Practical Statistics
- *Lecturer: Dr Andres Benchimol*
> [Predicting Brexit: A High-Dimensional Demographic Analysis](/projects/computing_for_practical_statistics.html)
- Handled high-dimensional data: addressed severe multicollinearity across 45+ socio-demographic variables by implementing Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and reduced the feature space into interpretable latent vectors while retaining more than 70% of the variance.
- Utilized hierarchical clustering to engineer new categorical features that captured regional demographic similarities, further refining predictive power of the dataset.
- Built and validated a Binomial Generalized Linear Model (GLM). Ensured the parameter interpretability and the model achieved a Pseudo R-squared of $0.85$. Delivered a robust and interpretable model that demonstrates the strong link between specific economic strata and political outcomes.

#### Stochastic Systems
- *Lecturer: Prof Terry Soo*
> [Centralized vs Individual Queues: Efficiency, Fairness, and Behavioral Trade-offs in Real World Settings]()
- Built and validated a discrete-event simulation study in R comparing one centralized queue feeding multiple servers vs multiple individual queues under matched arrival/service assumptions, quantifying impacts on mean waiting time and waiting-time variability.
- Established a baseline comparison between the two queuing systems, proved the classical pooling effect by simulations, and systematically tested how the pooling advantage changes across real-world settings by varying key assumptions (e.g., arrival intensity, service-time variability, and customer behaviors, etc)
